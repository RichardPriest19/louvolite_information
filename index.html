Company History

The Founding Years (1969-1970s)

In 1969, Mr. Reginald Allsopp opened a business making vertical blinds in a small space above a butcher's shop in Marple, Cheshire. This humble beginning marked the start of what would become a global leader in the window blind industry. In 1970, the business moved to a factory unit in Compstall Hall and registered the company name Louvolite, beginning to find success selling vertical louvre blinds through third-party retailers.

The company was officially incorporated on May 21, 1973, as Louvolite Fabrics Limited. By this time, Louvolite was already selling products to clients both in the UK and around the globe. In 1978, the company had progressed enough to design and build their own equipment for making textiles, purchasing looms and equipment to produce their very own woven textiles. The focus shifted toward designing and manufacturing component parts in-house, developing a wide range of resources including fabric weaving and coating capabilities.

Major Expansion (1980s)

The 1980s brought a decade of major changes for Louvolite and the industry. All operations moved to Hyde into larger premises where the engineering division could expand to design and create components and introduce injection moulding tools, allowing for greater flexibility, creativity and development. This period saw significant international expansion, with the company beginning to supply customers in Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, and the Middle East. Louvolite also opened its first depots in Singapore and Holland as a gateway into Continental Europe.

Innovation and Product Development (1990s-2000s)

Louvolite continued its commitment to innovation throughout the 1990s and 2000s. In 1994, the company launched its high-performance SPC¬Æ fabric range, featuring washable fabrics that reflected heat and light more effectively, helping to conserve energy year-round. These fabrics were also anti-glare, making them ideal for offices, schools, and commercial spaces.

In 2006, Louvolite introduced the Perfect Fit Screwless Fitting System, revolutionizing the ease of blind installation. The company also launched the Slimline Vogue¬Æ vertical headrail, featuring trendy new colors and a more organic form. To meet the growing demand for high-quality systems, Louvolite embarked on a radical course of designing, developing, and introducing automatic robotic assembly for components, revolutionizing production capability.

Modern Era (2010s-Present)

The 2010s saw continued technological advancement and product innovation. In 2012, Louvolite introduced Vision blinds, a unique two-layer blind system. The company invested in digital printing technology in 2014, expanding its design capabilities and customization options.

In 2015, Louvolite launched One Touch‚Ñ¢ technology, bringing motorization to its product range. This innovation allowed customers to control their blinds with greater convenience and ease.

In 2018, Louvolite introduced Allusion¬Æ Blinds at the R+T trade show in Stuttgart. Made up of a combination of sheer and opaque textured fabric, Allusion¬Æ Blinds offer gentle daylight filtering whilst retaining the view when open and provide access to outside space by walking through the blind with doors open. The product was a major success, featuring a versatile patented weave that allows light control while maintaining privacy, child-safe wand operation, and optional motorization with One Touch motors.

In response to the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, Louvolite created CoronaSafe, a treatment for their Carnival fabric to help eliminate the virus from the fabric's surface. The company also introduced Clearguard, a transparent protective fabric to help businesses and health centers continue to work safely with the public while socially distancing. That same year saw the launch of the Urban Shutters collection, a new line of striking polysilk vinyl shutters offering functional styling with contemporary flair.

Continued Growth and Expansion

On a regular basis over the last 20 years, Louvolite has increased square footage to allow expansion in areas of importance, particularly customer service to maintain high service levels as business demands grow. The latest phase involved the creation of a purpose-built facility next to the existing head office site in Hyde, Cheshire. The new building is around 50,000 square feet on a 3.15-acre site and houses the Engineering Division.

Today, Louvolite supplies to over 100 countries worldwide and remains wholly engaged in the design, manufacture, and distribution of window blind systems and fabrics. The company has evolved from being simply a supplier of blinds to becoming a fully-fledged manufacturer and distributor, producing systems, fabrics, and tooling for roller, vertical, roman, pleated, cellular, Vision, panel, venetian, and Allusion window blinds.

Legacy: Under the continued leadership of founder and chairman Reg Allsopp, Louvolite has transformed from a one-product business above a butcher's shop into a global manufacturing powerhouse. The company's commitment to progression, innovation, and UK-based manufacturing has remained constant throughout its 55+ year history.
